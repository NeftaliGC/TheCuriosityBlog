"Ponte pilas con la ciudad" and the travel to Hidalgo

 

Hello everyone! We ARRIVED in Mexico City last, wednesday TO JOIN THE PROGRAM “Ponte pilas con la ciudad”. The weather was sunny and warm. We traveled by car, it TOOK us four hours to get here, the trip was TIRING but we arrived safely. Now we are staying with some relatives, it's fine but AT night you hear gunshots

BY the moment we have already visited the memory and tolerance museum where a lecture on “battery recycling” took place, after that we HAD a tour AT the museum, IT TOOK US 3 hours. Finally, we HAD lunch, but we haven´t tasted the local tacos yet :c

We are taking part in the program “Ponte pilas con la ciudad”. WE PARTICIPATED in the lecture “dealt with the impact of batteries that are badly discarded”, in addition WE HELPED TO EXPLAIN the operation of some battery collection centers where the company “Imágenes y Muebles Urbanos” (IMU) collects the batteries every fifteen days.

We joined this program, because batteries are a major pollutant to the environment. We are going to take part in activities like handing out flyers, helping at lectures, collecting batteries from people and taking them to collection centers.

In the future we are going to visit the archaeological zone of Teotihuacan, and WE ARE visiting several places of culinary prestige; perhaps we are coming back next year. If all goes well.

Hi people! We arrive in Hidalgo the last week, we didn't have a car, so we traveled by bus for 1 hour, during our trip the weather was warm and a bit cloudy; For now, we are staying in a very nice and comfortable local hotel.

We have already visited Huasca de Ocampo, but we haven´t visited the Tula caves yet. We ATE at a local restaurant THE FOOD was very cheap and very tasty, after that we WENT TO THE tourist area where we found an environmental and social water saving program called "Cosecha de lluvia".

We are taking part in this program "Cosecha de lluvia", its objective is to improve the water supply in the low-income neighborhoods. IT IS ruled by the Secretary of Environment, through the General Direction of Coordination of Environmental Policies and Culture, during our stay we are going to take part in different activities like, RAISING money to the project, helping with the lists of NEIGHBORHOODS and talking to the project managers

Tomorrow we are going to visit the archaeological zone of Tula; maybe we are coming back next month to visit more places in Hidalgo.
